The Problem

A recovery gap, quietly costing millions of patients.

Physical therapy patients are sent home with exercise routines and a hope that they'll follow through correctly. Most don't — and the cost is reinjury, slower recovery, and lost trust in their own rehabilitation.

By The Numbers
70%

of physical therapy patients fail to complete prescribed at-home exercises [1].

35%

of those who do perform their exercises incorrectly — without real-time feedback to correct form [1].

50M

Americans seek physical therapy annually, accounting for over 300M sessions per year [2].

The continuity-of-care gap.

Physical therapy sessions happen in clinics, but recovery happens at home. The current standard relies on patient self-reporting and infrequent clinic check-ins — a system built on subjective evidence and best intentions.

Without objective monitoring, errors compound. A patient performing an exercise incorrectly for three weeks doesn't realize it until their next visit, by which point the damage to the joint, the wrong neuromuscular pattern, or the reinjury has already happened.

This is the gap our device addresses: bringing objective, sensor-based form assessment out of the clinic and into the patient's own living room.

The Landscape

What doesn't work, and why we built something new.

The wearable therapy market is fragmented into three categories — each with structural limitations that prevent them from serving home-based rehabilitation.

Fitness Trackers

Garmin · Whoop · Apple Watch

Track steps and heart rate, but lack the precision to measure joint angles or detect form errors. Their algorithms target general fitness — not slow, precise rehabilitation movements.

Performance Wearables

Villanova · Athos

Monitor muscle activation for athletes during sport, but lack joint angle tracking and form correction. Designed for muscle fatigue analysis, not rehabilitation protocols.

Smart Compression Sleeve

Our approach

Multi-sensor fusion (IMU + EMG + PPG) in a soft wearable, paired with a real-time form classifier. Affordable, comfortable, and built specifically for unsupervised rehabilitation.
