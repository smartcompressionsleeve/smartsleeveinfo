The Device

Multi-sensor fusion in a soft, wearable form.

Six sensors, two pods, one Random Forest. Designed for the patient's living room — not the lab bench.

Overview

A wearable, built deliberately.

The Smart Compression Sleeve combines multiple sensor modalities in a comfortable compression sleeve form factor. Each design decision balances accuracy, comfort, cost, and the reality of unsupervised use.

i.

Comfortable for 30+ minute sessions

A single-layer nylon-spandex pull-on sleeve carries two sensor pods mounted with adjustable straps — light, breathable, and easy to put on.

ii.

Real-time form feedback

Bicep curl classification runs continuously, with feedback delivered to a Flutter mobile app via Python bridge.

iii.

Replaceable, serviceable components

3D-printed sensor casings with sliding access for easy sensor or wire replacement.

The Sensors

Six sensors, three modalities.

The combination of inertial, electromyographic, and photoplethysmographic signals gives the classifier a complete picture of each rep — kinematics, muscle effort, and cardiovascular response.

ICM-20948 IMU × 2

Joint Kinematics

Two 9-DOF IMUs mounted on the upper arm and forearm, multiplexed through a TCA9548A I²C bus expander. Combined to compute elbow flexion angle in real time.

Form Factor

One sleeve, two pods.

The device is a single-layer nylon-spandex pull-on compression sleeve that carries two strap-mounted sensor pods — one on the upper arm, one on the forearm. Keeping the electronics in self-contained pods means the sleeve itself stays light, breathable, and easy to wash.
