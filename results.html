Sensor Validation

Three sensors, three time-aligned signals.

Before training any classifier, we verified that each sensor produces meaningful, readable signal aligned with the curl protocol. The trace below shows EMG activity, IMU angular velocity, and the PPG waveform from a single synchronized 10-second trial.

Figure 1 — Multi-sensor validation All three sensors captured simultaneously over a 10-second trial. EMG (top) shows a quiet baseline followed by clear activation bursts once the curl begins. IMU gyro Z (middle) traces a single smooth flexion–extension arc, peaking near 45 dps at mid-curl. PPG (bottom) sustains a clean, regular pulse waveform throughout, confirming reliable signal on all channels.
Classifier Performance

Four classes, cleanly separated.

A Random Forest classifier was trained on per-rep features extracted from IMU and EMG signals. Cross-validation was performed using GroupKFold by trial to prevent within-trial leakage between train and test sets.

Figure 2 — Confusion matrix across 5 folds Rows are the actual class, columns the predicted class; cells show rep counts with row-normalized percentages. Strong diagonal dominance across all four classes. The only notable confusion is Left-swinging predicted as Swinging (6 reps, 17%) — both share an exaggerated lateral motion — and a few Swinging reps predicted as Left-swinging (3 reps, 7%).

Interpretation

What the numbers mean.

  • Multi-sensor integration confirmed across IMU, EMG, and PPG channels — all three produce usable, time-aligned signal in a single synchronized trial.
  • The Random Forest classifier reliably distinguishes all four form classes, with sub-5% variance across folds (5-fold GroupKFold, trial-grouped to prevent leakage).
  • Closest confusion occurs between Swinging and Left-swinging — both share an exaggerated lateral motion pattern. Normal vs. Swinging shows the strongest separation.
  • Current dataset has limited subject diversity for some classes; multi-subject validation is in progress to characterize generalization to new users.
