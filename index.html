The Gap
70%

of physical therapy patients don't complete prescribed at-home exercises.

35%

of those who do perform exercises incorrectly — without real-time feedback.

50M

Americans seek physical therapy annually, with no objective home monitoring.

A recovery gap, quietly costing millions of patients.

Physical therapy patients are sent home with exercise routines and a hope that they'll follow through correctly. Current consumer wearables track steps and heart rate, but none can tell whether a single rehabilitation exercise was performed with proper form. Clinical-grade systems exist, but they live behind insurance walls and clinic doors.

The Smart Compression Sleeve bridges that gap — bringing objective sensor-based form assessment into a soft, accessible device that patients can actually wear at home, every day, without supervision.

The Device

Multi-sensor fusion in a soft, wearable form factor.

Six sensors integrated into a three-layer compression sleeve, streaming data through an onboard microcontroller to a real-time machine learning classifier — all powered by a rechargeable battery small enough to disappear into the fabric.

i.

Dual ICM-20948 IMUs

Joint angle tracking via fused upper-arm and forearm orientation through an I²C multiplexer.

ii.

DFRobot Analog EMG

Muscle activation detection on the biceps brachii via dry surface electrodes.

iii.

DFRobot PPG

Continuous heart rate monitoring to flag cardiovascular strain during rehab.

iv.

Adafruit Feather nRF52840

Wired serial streaming to a Python bridge for feature extraction and live ML inference.

How It Works

From skin to real-time feedback, in under 150 ms.

i.

Sensing

Six sensors capture biomechanical and physiological signals from the upper arm and forearm simultaneously.

  • IMU × 2 (I²C mux)
  • Analog EMG on A1
  • Analog PPG for HR
ii.

Processing

An Adafruit Feather streams synchronized sensor packets over wired serial to a Python bridge that segments reps and extracts features.

  • Adafruit nRF52840
  • Python WebSocket bridge
  • Per-rep feature vector
iii.

Classification

A Random Forest classifier — trained on 417 labeled reps — predicts the form class and routes feedback to a Flutter mobile companion app.

  • Random Forest (sklearn)
  • 4 form classes
  • Flutter + Supabase
